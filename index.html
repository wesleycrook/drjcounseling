ABOUT

My Story

My therapeutic approach begins by offering clients a safe place to process the difficulties and stressors in their personal and professional lives and to assist them with finding their own solutions. My specialties include working with clients living with depression, anxiety, and grief as well as families and care partners caring for a loved one living with dementia. I am also a facilitator for Prepare-Enrich, an evidence-based assessment tool that helps couples gain insight into the dynamics of their relationship by identifying areas of strength as well as areas of potential growth.

I received a Bachelor of Science degree in Public and Social Services in 1995 from Kennesaw State University, Kennesaw, Georgia. I subsequently earned a Masters of Education in Professional Counseling from Augusta State University, Augusta, GA, in 2005, and my professional counseling license in 2009. I am with licensure in Georgia and Arizona.
